Building a recommender system from scratch¶

Step 3 Pre-processing¶

What if we built a movie recommender? In order to do this we would have to gather 4 things.

  1. Movies classified by their levels of Action, Comedy, Drama, etc ranging from 0 to 10 for example link to the article
  2. A database of people together with their movie reviews ie. likes, comments, shares link to the article
  3. Pre-process the data so that it can be fed into existing sci-kit learn libraries current article
  4. Last but not least we need a good grasp of the statistical (or deep learning) concepts we want to apply Christopher Fonnesbeck's video is a good introduction to statistical modeling with Python if you're just starting out

The target value we are trying to predict is the movie we would recommend based on movie preferences. Movie preference can be infered through movie reviews in step 2.

Based on these fake user reviews you can see how we will go about building our recommender. We look at the ratings given to Indiana Jones and compute the correlation with the other movies in the set. Indiana Jones is very similar to Star wars and inception because users rated them very similarly ie. these movie vectors are highly correlated cor 1

Similarity is given by the corrwith function which is the covariance of both distributions divided by the standard deviation of both distributions

cor(X, Y) = Cov(X, Y)/sd(X)*sd(Y)

The standard deviation is a measure of variance in the distribution ie. the sum of the distances between the samples and the mean.

{% load static%}

The covariance on the other hand gives us the amount of variation between X and Y as well as the relationship between the distributions ie. whether there is a positive relationship or a negative one.
